Welcome to Boston

The City of Boston is committed to combating climate change through various initiatives, including energy-efficient building codes, improved public transportation, and renewable energy adoption, with a goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2050. In 2018, the city's emissions from buildings and transportation increased by 4%, reaching 6.4 million metric tons of greenhouse gases. This rise was driven by higher electricity and natural gas use, as well as a 9% increase in vehicle miles traveled.

Despite this increase, Boston has achieved significant emissions reductions. In 2018, emissions were 20% lower than in 2005 with renewable energy credits (RECs) and 19% lower without RECs. Local government operations reduced emissions by 37% compared to 2005 with RECs and 30% without RECs. Boston achieved its 2020 goal of cutting municipal emissions 25% below 2005 levels in 2015, five years ahead of schedule. Notably, as the city's population and job opportunities grew, emissions per resident decreased by 39%, from 15 to 9 metric tons per year. Boston's economic growth, measured by Gross City Product (GCP), increased from 106 billion dollars to 129 billion, with emissions per million dollars of GCP decreasing by 35%, from 76 to 49 metric tons per million dollars.
